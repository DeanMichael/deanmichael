GitHub

After years of operating in startup environments, my core learning at GitHub was how to remain effective in a large team environment where more people, opinions, and process add significantly more complexity to even the simplest of projects.

At Nano, I learned how to balance selling 'the vision' vs. 'the now'. The tech was incredibly unique and ground-breaking, but their machine came at a very high cost and low rate of success, leaving me with a very small pool of early adopters.

Cartesian was a small 3D print lab I co-founded with a couple friends. Here I learned the complexities of being an owner-operator of a small business, while also running a very resource constrained sales effort.

Make Playdate, Inc.

While being responsible for the growth of playdate, I learned the value of doing things that don't always scale early on, and extracting information from those activities that will ultimately drive efficiencies moving forward.

GoEngineer

I was hired to create a new role for GoEngineer. Before I joined, they did not run any outbound sales efforts. Here I learned how to be creative in my approach to sales without any playbook, and systematizing what worked.

Acquisition Entrepreneurship

Over the last couple years I taught myself how to navigate the world of SMB acquisitions. Below are 5 deals that I brought to contract, but never closed - click each to learn more.

$2.8M Offer: Marketing Attribution Co.

Revenue: $1.4M | SDE: $750K | Outcome: Outbid
High growth, high margin, but also high customer concentration. My offer was for 80% of the business, while the owner would retain 20%. He received multiple offers, some of which were more than double mine.

$1.3M Offer: Transmission Repair Shop

Revenue: $1.25M | SDE: $350K | Outcome: Seller withdrew
Long term successful shop, under a franchise label and the deal included real estate. Seller pulled out for family health reasons.

$730K Offer: Marketing SaaS Platform

Revenue: $575K | SDE: $225K | Outcome: Seller withdrew
This business offered compliance software for email marketers. During due diligence I found a very low usage rate of their product and hedged against that in the contract. Seller did not want to agree to the terms, signaling major risk to revenue.

$450K Offer: SaaS for Law Practices

Revenue: $375K | SDE: $280K | Outcome: Outbid
This business offered software to track timelines of cases, enabling attorneys to present more comprehensively in court. This was a great opportunity and I was pretty disappointed I was not able to compete with other bidders.

$140K Offer: Audio Visual Services Provider

Revenue: $315K | SDE: $120K | Outcome: Buyer withdrew
I like the AV industry, everything is pretty straightforward and it's fractured enough to pursue a consolidation play. This deal however, fell apart when the seller breached the terms of the LOI and withheld critical records.
